ARITHMETIC SEQUENCE

DEFINITION

An arithmetic sequence is where the difference (d) between any two consecutive terms is always constant.

The nth term of the arithmetic sequence with the first term a1 and the common difference (d) is given by the following formula.

KEEP IN MIND:

an = nth term

a1 = 1st term

d = common difference

n = total no. of terms

MATHEMATICAL PROBLEM 1

a.) How much money will he have saved on the 7th day?

We’re going to use the formula that we have for an arithmetic sequence.

aₙ = a₁ + (n - 1) d

Substitute the terms

a₇ = 50 + (7 - 1) 3

First, do the equation inside of the parenthesis (7-1) which equates to 6

a₇ = 50 + (6) 3

Multiply 6 and 3 afterwards which will equal 18

a₇ = 50 + 18

Add both 50 and 18 which equals 68 and that’s your final answer!

a₇ = 50 + 18 = 68

FINAL ANSWER: Zephyr saved 68 pesos on the 7th day

b.) What is the total amount of money saved after 7 days?

This question asks for the total sum of Zephyr’s money after 7 days. Which is asking for an arithmetic series, we’re going to use the following formula:

Sₙ = n2 ∙ [ 2a₁ + (n-1) ∙ d ]

Substitute the terms based on our given

S₇ = 72 ∙ [ 2 (50) + (7-1) ∙ 3 ]

First, do what’s inside of the parenthesis which is (7-1) and that equals 6

S₇ = 72 ∙ [ 2 (50) + (6) ∙ 3 ]

Next, multiply 2 by 50 which equals 100

S₇ = 72 ∙ [ 100 + (6) ∙ 3 ]

Multiply 6 and 3 and that equals 18

S₇ = 72 ∙ (100 + 18)

Combine both terms (100 + 18) which is equal to 118

S₇ = 7/2 ∙ 118/1

Multiply 118 to 7/2 by adding a denominator of 1 under 118 to multiply. After that simply 826/2 which makes our final answer 413

S₇ = 7/2 ∙ 118/1 → S₇ = 826/2

S₇ = 413

FINAL ANSWER: The total amount of money Zephyr saved is 413 pesos after 7 days.

MATHEMATICAL PROBLEM 2

We’re going to use this formula to find the sum of the arithmetic sequence

Sₙ = n/2 ∙ [ 2 a₁ + (n-1) ∙ d ]

Substitute the terms into the formula

S₆ = 6/2 ∙ [ 2 (20) + (6-1) ∙ 5 ]

Subtract 6 and 1 because they’re in a parenthesis. And we’re following the rule of PEMDAS throughout this whole solution

S₆ = 6/2 ∙ [ 2 (20) + (5) ∙ 5 ]

Multiply 2 by 20 which equals 40 and multiply 5 and 5 and the product is 25

S₆ = 6/2 ∙ (40 + 25)

Combine 40 and 25 and that equals 65

S₆ = 6/2 ∙ 65

Simplify 6/2 which is 3

S₆ = 3 ∙ 65

Multiply 3 and 65 and that makes our final answer 195

S₆ = 195

FINAL ANSWER: After 6 weeks, Sarah will have saved 195 pesos.

MATHEMATICAL PROBLEM 3

Using this formula:

Sₙ = n/2 ∙ [ 2 a₁ + (n-1) ∙ d ]

We substitute the formula with the given variables in the problem.

S₁₀ = 10/2 ∙ [2 (500) + (10-1) ∙ 40 ]

We first solve for what is inside the parentheses, 10-1 = 9. Multiply 2 to 500 which will equal to 1000

S₁₀ = 10/2 ∙ [1000 + (9) ∙ 40]

We then multiply 9 and 40 with the utilization of PEMDAS which will equal 360

S₁₀ = 10/2 ∙ (1000 + 360)

Add both terms in the parenthesis, which are 1,000 and 360, then divide with the quotient of 10 divided by 2

S₁₀ = 5 ∙ 1360

Finally, we multiply 5 and 1360 to get the sum of the distance of the first ten echoes
